About HomeEnabled

We believe growing older should never mean giving up the home you love — or your say in how you live in it.

Our Story

HomeEnabled began in 2021 with a stack of confusing brochures on a kitchen table. Our founder was helping his mother — recently widowed, fiercely independent, and recovering from a hip replacement — figure out whether a stair lift, a ramp, or a first-floor remodel would let her stay in the house where she'd raised her family. The information available was either a sales pitch or a medical journal. Nothing in between.

So we built the in-between. HomeEnabled is an independent review site covering the products, technology and health-care developments that make aging in place possible: stair lifts, walk-in tubs, lift chairs, mobility scooters, ramps, and the steady stream of innovations arriving every year. We do the research, talk to the experts, dig through owner experiences, and write it all up in plain English.

How We Review

  • Expert-led: our review team includes an occupational therapist and a Certified Aging-in-Place Specialist (CAPS), and every guide is fact-checked before publication.
  • Independent: brands cannot pay for placement, scores or rankings. Ever.
  • Reader-supported: we earn affiliate commissions from retailers such as AmeriGlide and US Medical Supplies when you buy through our links — at no extra cost to you. That's the whole business model, disclosed on every page.
  • Human: we review for real households, real budgets and real bodies — not spec sheets.

Meet the Team

Daniel Brooks, OT — Occupational therapist focused on fall prevention and adaptive equipment. Covers bathroom safety and mobility.

A Note on Medical Advice

HomeEnabled provides product research and education, not medical advice. Every body and every home is different — before making significant purchases, we encourage a conversation with your physician or an occupational therapist who can assess your specific situation.
